HISTORY OF ILIGAN

Spanish colonial era

The Spanish colonization of the Philippines began in the 16th century, but the Iligan area remained relatively untouched until the latter part of the 19th century. Spanish authorities established a military fort in Iligan in 1850, primarily to protect against pirate attacks along the coastline.

-

Post-war Reconstruction and Development

After World War II, Iligan City underwent a period of reconstruction and rapid development. The city's economy diversified with the establishment of industries such as steel manufacturing, hydroelectric power generation, and agribusiness. These industries contributed to Iligan's growth as an industrial center in Mindanao.

-

Cityhood and Modern Era

Iligan was officially declared a city on June 16, 1950, through Republic Act No. 525. Over the decades, the city continued to thrive, attracting migrants from other parts of the Philippines in search of employment opportunities and a better quality of life. Today, Iligan City is known for its vibrant culture, natural attractions, and dynamic economy, with industries ranging from manufacturing to tourism contributing to its prosperity.

Throughout its history, Iligan City has endured challenges and overcome obstacles, emerging as a resilient and thriving urban center in Mindanao, while still preserving its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.
